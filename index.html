The Problem

Why Are So Many Ontario Families Missing Out?

The RDSP has existed since 2008. Yet the vast majority of eligible Canadians have never opened one. Here's why.

❓

They Don't Know It Exists

The RDSP is rarely discussed by banks, doctors, or social workers. Most families only hear about it by chance — often years after becoming eligible.

😰

They're Afraid of Losing Benefits

A common myth is that RDSP savings will reduce ODSP payments. In Ontario, this is completely false. The RDSP is 100% exempt from ODSP asset and income tests.

🧩

The Rules Seem Complicated

The 10-year repayment rule, carry-forward provisions, and grant matching tiers can feel overwhelming. A knowledgeable advisor makes it simple.

Why It Matters

Six Reasons to Open an RDSP Today

1

Government Matches Up to 300%

For every $1,500 you contribute, the government may add $3,500 — before any investment growth.

2

Tax-Sheltered Growth

All investment growth inside the RDSP is tax-free until withdrawal, compounding your wealth significantly over time.

3

ODSP Is Fully Protected

In Ontario, RDSP assets and withdrawals are 100% exempt from ODSP calculations. Your support payments are safe.

4

Catch Up on Missed Years

If you were DTC-eligible in prior years, you may claim up to 10 years of retroactive grants in a single year.

5

Anyone Can Contribute

Family members, friends, and even employers can contribute to the RDSP with the beneficiary's written consent.

6

Free Money Requires No Contribution

Lower-income families receive the $1,000 annual CDSB bond with zero personal contribution required.
